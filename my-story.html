my story

I wanted to learn how to code because I love the idea that technology can solve social problems. I also wanted to have the skills to be able to create any app or website imaginable. I came to this careerpath in a non-traditional way, as I studied the humanities in college. I actually thought I was going to be a history professor! I wrote a dissertation on the impacts of US militarism on women in the Philippines (which probably only six people have read, but if you're interested you can read more here).

While I really enjoyed academic work, especially the creativity involved in pursuing a unique research project, I also felt like something was missing for me. Much of the work I was doing felt abstract, and generally it takes many years to see results (historians often spend decades working on a book before it gets published). This is what eventually led me to pursue UX design and web development.
